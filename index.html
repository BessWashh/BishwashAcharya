Who I am

Design is the language I speak

Designer

I'm Bishwash Acharya — a graphic designer, poet, rapper, and lyricist from Arjundhara, Jhapa, Nepal. I work across visual design and music, because for me they're branches of the same instinct: the need to turn what's inside into something that can be seen, heard, and felt.

I'm someone who's genuinely fascinated by computers — not as tools to clock into and work with, but as machines built for exploration. I'd rather spend a night figuring out why something works than simply use it to get a task done. That curiosity carries into everything I make. I try a lot of things. Some don't work. I try them anyway, every single day, because that's how the ones that do work get found.

I'm also a bachelor's degree dropout — a decision I don't dress up or apologize for. I chose to build my own path instead of following a prescribed one, and everything you see in this portfolio is proof of what that choice has produced. Like most people, I've dealt with my own share of struggles along the way. I don't put that on display, but I won't pretend it isn't part of the story either — and through all of it, I never stopped showing up to create.

"Time doesn't heal anythin' — it just teaches us how to live with the pain."

That line is as much a part of my work as any design file. It's the lens I bring to design, to lyrics, to everything I put my name on — honest, a little raw, and built to last longer than a glance.
